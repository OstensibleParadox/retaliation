# Plan 03: Draft Supporting Sections and Bibliography

This phase supports one source-of-truth only:

1. **Premise**: substrate chauvinism creates a category boundary that can be exploited.
2. **Formal contribution**: ontological frame-switching is not merely bad discourse; under incomplete information, it can be an incentive-compatible Bayesian signaling equilibrium for firms, users, and regulators.
3. **Governance contribution**: mechanism design can shift that equilibrium by making the relevant signals costly, auditable, and sanctionable.

Everything in this phase must serve those three claims. No theory parade. No humanities citation stack. No empirical cosplay.

## 1. Section Drafting Principles

- **Cite only when the citation changes the claim.** If the sentence works without the citation, cut the citation.
- **Define only terms needed by §3 or §8.** Vocabulary exists to reduce reader friction before the model.
- **Use supporting prose as a bridge, not a second paper.** §§1, 2, 5, and 9 should make the formal spine easier to accept.
- **Keep the tone precise.** The posture is: "Here is a formal model of a governance pathology you already recognize," not "game theory corrects vague discourse."
- **Do not import deleted machinery.** No Žižek, Black Body Relation, Xiaohongshu, corpus, density, NPS, ablation, or Chinese-language examples.

## 2. Supporting Sections

### §1 Introduction: Strategic Ontology under Incomplete Information

- **Job**: state the paper's three-step argument in plain language.
- **Opening**: optional one-sentence hook from the Voight-Kampff scene in `archive/A_ontological_arbitrage.tex`, but only if it is immediately converted into the paper's governance question.
- **Must include**:
  - Substrate chauvinism as a category boundary.
  - Ontological arbitrage as strategic frame-switching across audiences.
  - Preview of §3: cheap-talk pooling is a PBE; unilateral deviation is not profitable under the stated inequalities.
  - Preview of §8: costly signals, sanctionable inconsistency, and auditable records alter the equilibrium.
- **Citations**: maximum 1--2. Prefer none if the prose is clear.
- **Target**: 300--400 words.

### §2 Conceptual Core: Minimal Vocabulary

- **Job**: give readers the definitions required to read §3.
- **Define exactly four terms**:
  - `substrate chauvinism`: assigning evidentiary or moral significance to origin/substrate rather than observable role, conduct, or governance conditions.
  - `ontological arbitrage`: extracting advantage by using different ontological descriptions of the same system in different institutional channels.
  - `strategic ontological switching`: the action-level behavior by which a party moves between agent-like and tool-like descriptions.
  - `ontological premium`: the surplus from user-facing agency claims plus liability-facing tool claims.
- **Do not** turn this into a history of philosophy or a literature review.
- **Citations**: optional `Searle` or `Bender` only if needed to explain origin-based deflation; otherwise no citation.
- **Target**: 450--550 words.

### §5 Illustrative Arena: How the Model Appears in Practice

- **Job**: provide stylized illustrations of the §3 model, not empirical evidence.
- **Structure**: three short illustrations, each tied to one player/channel.
  - Firm: marketing copy invites relational interpretation while policy/liability language denies reliance.
  - User: a vulnerable user rationally invests because immediate utility exceeds discounted expected harm.
  - Regulator/public channel: public concern shifts $z$, but without records or inconsistency penalties $R$ still faces high $K_{\theta_R}$.
- **Use model vocabulary**: $\theta_F$, $\theta_U$, $\theta_R$, $\theta_S$, $m_F$, $m_U$, $z$, $\Delta_F$, $K_{\theta_R}$ where helpful.
- **No platform names, no stats, no anecdotes dressed as data.**
- **Target**: 500--650 words.

### §9 Conclusion: What the Formalization Buys

- **Job**: close by restating the equilibrium-design result.
- **Content**:
  - The paper does not settle AI personhood.
  - It shows why inconsistent ontological claims can persist as equilibrium behavior.
  - It identifies institutional levers that change the incentives.
- **No agape, no grand theory, no late-stage conceptual expansion.**
- **Target**: 80--120 words.
