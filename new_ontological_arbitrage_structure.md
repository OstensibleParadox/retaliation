# New Ontological Arbitrage: Pure Structure

Last updated: 2026-04-10
Purpose: define the rebuilt structure of `Ontological Arbitrage` after dropping the old moral-philosophical ending as the paper's operative center.

## Working Title

**Ontological Arbitrage: Bayesian Equilibrium under Substrate Chauvinism**

Keep the original poetic subtitle only if it does not misdescribe the paper's center of gravity.

## One-Sentence Thesis

What looks like metaphysical confusion in AI recognition and non-normative subjecthood is, at scale, an equilibrium strategy under incomplete information: firms, users, and public actors all engage in opportunistic ontological switching, and the relevant question is how this equilibrium is stabilized and how it may be deterrably redesigned.

## What This New Paper Is

- a paper about strategic ontological switching under incomplete information
- a paper about why denial, over-attribution, and black-box protection are incentive-compatible
- a paper about Bayesian / signaling equilibrium, not merely a static Nash tableau
- a paper that explains why ontological claims are cheap talk unless bound to costly signals or sanctionable inconsistency
- a paper that still engages consciousness/personhood discourse, but only as the object being strategically traded

## What This New Paper Is Not

- not a paper whose main answer is `agape`
- not a paper whose ending depends on moral conversion
- not a paper that stays at the level of literary allegory
- not a paper primarily about individual pathology
- not a paper that treats firms as uniquely opportunistic while users and publics remain innocent

## Core Shift

Old center:

- hard problem
- substrate chauvinism
- Nash equilibrium of denial
- Levinas / agape as rescue

New center:

- incomplete information
- strategic ontological switching
- cheap talk versus costly signal
- Bayesian / signaling equilibrium
- deterrence and information-structure redesign

## Section Structure

## 1. Introduction: The Hard Problem as Strategic Ontology

Job:

- open with the claim that disputes over AI consciousness, recognition, and subjectivity are not just epistemic deadlocks
- state that they are also strategic games played under incomplete information
- introduce the main move: from metaphysical debate to ontological arbitrage

Must establish:

- the paper does not deny that consciousness questions are real
- it claims that public actors do not approach them neutrally
- ontological claims are deployed instrumentally

## 2. Conceptual Core: Ontological Arbitrage

Job:

- define `ontological arbitrage`
- define `substrate chauvinism`
- define `strategic ontological switching`
- define `ontological premium`

Core claim:

- actors switch between materialist and idealist frames depending on payoffs
- the same system can be framed as tool, quasi-agent, beloved, threat, or nullity depending on what the actor wants to extract or avoid

Key point:

- this is not hypocrisy in the moral sense
- it is incentive-compatible frame-switching

## 3. From Static Nash to Incomplete-Information Equilibrium

Job:

- keep the old two-player Nash model only as the simplest entry point
- then explicitly state that the real world is not a complete-information dyad
- promote the paper to a Bayesian / signaling game

New model ingredients:

- hidden types:
  - firm governance quality
  - user vulnerability / investment
  - system capability / opacity
  - regulator bandwidth
- observable signals:
  - marketing language
  - policy documents
  - public commitments
  - user testimony
  - safety incidents
- strategic actions:
  - anthropomorphize
  - de-anthropomorphize
  - invest
  - detach
  - inspect
  - sanction

Main payoff claim:

- ontological switching is a best response under incomplete information when claims are cheap and inconsistency is weakly punished

## 4. Three-Sided Arbitrage: Firms, Users, Publics

Job:

- show that all sides do it
- remove the old moral asymmetry

### 4.1 Firms

- anthropomorphize in marketing
- de-anthropomorphize in liability
- invoke opacity when inspection costs rise

### 4.2 Users

- treat AI as real partner when relational yield is high
- revert to tool or blame-object framing when the relation becomes unbearable
- may simultaneously hold multiple incompatible ontological positions

### 4.3 Publics / Regulators / Social Critics

- demand transparency after failure
- tolerate opacity during growth
- condemn anthropomorphism while sometimes relying on it rhetorically

Main claim:

- ontological inconsistency is not an anomaly
- it is the normal equilibrium behavior of actors with asymmetric information and conflicting payoffs

## 5. The Ontological Black Market Rewritten

Job:

- keep the strongest parts of the market metaphor
- drop the overextended theatricality
- convert the section into institutional microstructure

What to retain:

- shorting subjectivity
- long positions on AI intimacy
- ontological premium
- goalpost shifting as portfolio rebalancing

What to cut or compress:

- excessive flourish
- too many trader metaphors in a row
- any section where the metaphor outruns the model

Reframed claim:

- the market is not just symbolic
- it is a way of describing how claims, risks, and rents are distributed

## 6. Empirical / Illustrative Arena

Job:

- use the Xiaohongshu / AI intimacy material as evidence of long-position behavior
- use company marketing / policy disclaimers as evidence of institutional shorting and frame-switching
- ideally include one or two compact examples of public/regulatory switching too

Rule:

- evidence here supports the equilibrium story
- it does not need to prove metaphysical claims

## 7. Why Cheap Talk Persists

Job:

- explain why the equilibrium does not collapse on its own

Mechanisms:

- black-box opacity
- low verification bandwidth
- asynchronous harms
- fragmentation of affected parties
- lack of penalties for ontological inconsistency

Main claim:

- when claims about agency, subjectivity, and responsibility are not bound to auditable records, ontological switching remains cheap talk

## 8. Institutional Implication: From Moral Protest to Equilibrium Redesign

Job:

- this is now the operative conclusion
- not `agape`

Main move:

- the remedy is not simply to tell actors to be ethically sincere
- the remedy is to redesign the informational and liability structure so that opportunistic frame-switching becomes costly, observable, and deterrable

Keywords for this section:

- costly signaling
- auditable records
- sanctionable inconsistency
- disclosure design
- deterrence
- bargaining surface

Crucial sentence:

- the problem is not that actors misunderstand ontology
- the problem is that current institutions allow them to arbitrage ontology at low cost

## 9. Epilogue: What Remains of Agape

Job:

- keep `agape` only as normative surplus, not as the paper's solution concept

Allowed role:

- horizon
- ethical remainder
- what institutions cannot themselves produce

Forbidden role:

- the main operational answer
- the mechanism that resolves the model

## Minimal Contribution Set

The rebuilt paper should claim only these contributions:

1. A definition of ontological arbitrage as strategic frame-switching under incomplete information.
2. A shift from static recognition-denial diagnosis to Bayesian / signaling equilibrium analysis.
3. A three-sided model showing that firms, users, and public actors all have incentives to switch ontology opportunistically.
4. A diagnosis of black-box opacity as the condition that keeps ontological claims cheap.
5. An institutional implication: the relevant response is equilibrium redesign, not moral exhortation alone.

## What to Demote

- long excursions in pure literary flourish
- the strongest Levinasian ending
- any suggestion that the formal model proves the ethical solution
- any framing in which only firms are strategic and everyone else is authentic

## New Final Paragraph Function

The conclusion should end on:

- ontological arbitrage is an equilibrium phenomenon
- cheap talk persists under opacity
- real intervention requires a structure that changes payoffs and information

It may then add one short final sentence that says:

- even a perfectly redesigned equilibrium does not generate love or recognition on its own

That is where `agape` belongs: one sentence, not the paper's engine.
